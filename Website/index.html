Konoha

Land of fire

Konoha Village, also known as the Hidden Leaf Village, is a prominent fictional village in the Naruto series created by Masashi Kishimoto. It is the main setting for much of the story and serves as the home of many iconic ninja characters. Konoha is known for its strong ninja traditions, breathtaking architecture, and the iconic Hokage Monument, which honors the village's powerful leaders.
